## ENGINEERING LAB

**Intro:** This is where I do most of my thinking — with my hands. My favorite builds cross disciplines: a physical machine made smarter with code. Each project below started as a question ("could I…?") and turned into something real — and the newest category points to where I'm heading next.

### Bluetooth-Controlled Lego EV3 Car — *Robotics*
**Summary:** A remote-controlled car built on the Lego Mindstorms EV3, with a stable Bluetooth link to an external controller.
- **Challenge:** Program the EV3 Intelligent Brick to establish and hold a reliable Bluetooth connection with a phone/tablet controller.
- **Build:** Assembled the chassis for structural integrity under quick starts and stops, tuning gear ratios for the right balance of speed and torque.
- **Skills:** Robotics control · wireless communication · hardware/software debugging.

### Upcycled RC Glider — *Aeronautics*
**Summary:** A flyable glider built from salvaged RC-car electronics and a hand-made cardboard airframe.
- **Challenge:** Apply real aeronautical principles while keeping the build sustainable — reusing a discarded RC car's motor, battery, and electronics.
- **Build:** Crafted custom wings and stabilizers from lightweight cardboard; iterated repeatedly to perfect the airfoil and center of gravity; soldered and wired the salvaged parts into the new frame.
- **Skills:** Aerodynamics · weight distribution · material science · soldering · iterative design.

### Arduino Steering Wheel (Unojoy) — *Microcontrollers*
**Summary:** A custom steering wheel that interfaces with PC games, built on an Arduino Uno.
- **Challenge:** Read analog rotation, convert it to digital data, and present it to a computer as a standard game controller.
- **Build:** Wrote C++ in the Arduino IDE using the Unojoy framework to map a potentiometer's rotation and custom buttons to controller axes.
- **Skills:** Microcontrollers · analog-to-digital I/O · device interfacing.

### Lego Master Builds — *Structures*
**Summary:** A history of complex, large-scale Lego builds — my earliest engineering training.
- **Focus:** Mechanical systems, structural stability, and architectural design.
- **Skills:** Spatial reasoning · following intricate instructions · patience · attention to detail.

**Building now / next:**
- **3D CAD & Printing** — designing and printing mechanical parts in Fusion 360 on a Bambu Lab printer.
- **Smart-Home Automation** — using our home Proxmox server, Home Assistant, and Z-Wave for robotic/drone automation.
- **FIRST Lego League sub-club** — structuring and pitching a new FLL club with Mr. Hahn (awaiting funding approval).

**AI & Autonomy — where I'm heading** *(forward-looking category; keeps the architecture open and signals the integration direction):*
- **Autonomous Drone (planned)** — combining my flight, electronics, and coding work into a self-stabilizing, sensor-driven aircraft. Skills: sensors, control loops, Python.
